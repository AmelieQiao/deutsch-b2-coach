## Recommended baseline assessment kit (best materials + order)

Goal: a baseline that is **exam-like**, spans **A2→B2**, and stays within **~30 minutes** total working time.

Recommended order:
1. **A2 exam sampling**: `materials/A2/exams/A2_Modellsatz_Erwachsene.pdf`
   - Purpose: quickly detect missing foundations and prevent a B1/B2-only baseline from misreading your level.
2. **B1 exam core**: `materials/B1/exams/b1_modellsatz_erwachsene.pdf`
   - Purpose: measure your bridge stability in reading/writing/grammar-in-context (often the decisive step to B2 readiness).
3. **B2 exam sampling (target gap probe)**: `materials/B2/exams/b2_modellsatz_erwachsene.pdf`
   - Purpose: measure “distance to target scope” using a small, controlled sample.
4. **Support references during analysis (not during the test)**:
   - A2 grammar map: `materials/A2/grammar/A2-德语A2知识点总结_20250307193752.pdf`
   - B1 drill source: `materials/B1/grammar/B1-Grammatik aktiv A1-B1 neu-Friederike Jin Ute Voß_*.pdf`
   - Quick recall summary: `materials/B1/grammar/B1-语法总结.pdf`
   - Wordlists (for building your post-baseline vocab plan): `materials/A2/vocab/A2_Wortliste_Goethe.pdf`, `materials/B1/vocab/B1_Wortliste_Goethe.pdf`, `materials/B2/vocab/B2-Goethe-Zertifikat_B2_Wortliste.pdf`

Notes:
- During baseline, we should avoid “open-book fixing”; we use references **after** to categorize errors and plan the smallest next step.
- If B2 sampling is clearly far above comfort level, we shorten it and invest the time into pinpointing the exact A2/B1 gaps instead.
